**Effective Date:** {DATE}

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes workforce security requirements to ensure that all individuals with access to electronic protected health information (ePHI) are authorized, trained, and supervised, in compliance with HIPAA Security Rule 164.308(a)(3).

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all workforce members of {ORGANIZATION_NAME}, including full-time and part-time employees, contractors, temporary staff, volunteers, and any other person whose conduct is under the direct control of the organization, whether or not they are paid.

## 3. Hiring and Authorization — 164.308(a)(3)(ii)(A)

### 3.1 Background Checks

- Background checks must be completed before granting access to ePHI systems.
- Checks must include, at minimum: {BACKGROUND_CHECK_SCOPE} (e.g., criminal history, employment verification, OIG/SAM exclusion list).
- Background checks are conducted by {BACKGROUND_CHECK_VENDOR}.
- Results are reviewed by {HR_CONTACT} and {SECURITY_OFFICER} before access is provisioned.
- Positions with elevated access to ePHI require additional screening as determined by {SECURITY_OFFICER}.

### 3.2 Access Authorization

- Access to ePHI systems must be formally authorized before provisioning.
- The employee's manager must submit an access request specifying the role, systems, and level of access required.
- {SECURITY_OFFICER} must approve all access requests based on the minimum necessary principle.
- Access must not be granted until the employee has completed initial HIPAA training.

## 4. HIPAA Training Requirements — 164.308(a)(5)

### 4.1 Initial Training

All workforce members must complete HIPAA security and privacy training:

- Within {INITIAL_TRAINING_DEADLINE} of hire date (maximum 30 days).
- Before being granted access to any system containing ePHI.
- Training must cover: HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules, organization policies, acceptable use, incident reporting, phishing awareness, physical security, and sanctions for violations.

### 4.2 Annual Refresher Training

- All workforce members with ePHI access must complete annual refresher training by {ANNUAL_TRAINING_DEADLINE} each year.
- Refresher training must cover any policy changes, recent incidents or industry breaches, and emerging threats.

### 4.3 Role-Specific Training

- Workforce members in IT, security, or administrative roles with elevated access must receive additional training on: system administration security, audit log review, incident response procedures, and encryption key management.
- Training must occur within 30 days of role assignment.

### 4.4 Training Records

- Training completion records must be maintained in {TRAINING_RECORD_SYSTEM}.
- Records must include: employee name, training date, topics covered, and acknowledgment signature.
- Training records must be retained for a minimum of 6 years.
- {HR_CONTACT} must track compliance and escalate non-completion to management.

## 5. Acceptable Use

Workforce members with access to ePHI must:

- Access only the minimum ePHI necessary for their job function.
- Never share credentials, access tokens, or authentication devices.
- Never copy ePHI to personal devices, personal email, or unauthorized cloud services.
- Lock workstations when leaving them unattended.
- Report suspected security incidents or policy violations immediately.
- Use only {ORGANIZATION_NAME}-approved devices and software for accessing ePHI.
- Comply with all related policies (Access Control, Encryption, Audit Logging).

## 6. Sanctions Policy — 164.308(a)(1)(ii)(C)

### 6.1 Sanctions for Violations

{ORGANIZATION_NAME} applies sanctions against workforce members who violate security policies:

| Violation Severity | Examples | Sanction |
|-------------------|---------|---------|
| Minor (first offense) | Unlocked workstation, late training completion | Verbal warning and documented counseling |
| Moderate | Sharing credentials, unauthorized ePHI access without malicious intent | Written warning, mandatory retraining |
| Serious | Repeated violations, negligent ePHI exposure, refusal to complete training | Suspension, access revocation, performance review |
| Severe | Intentional unauthorized access, ePHI theft, sabotage, selling PHI | Immediate termination, legal referral, law enforcement notification |

### 6.2 Sanctions Process

1. Violation is reported to {SECURITY_OFFICER} and {HR_CONTACT}.
2. Investigation is conducted and documented.
3. Sanction is determined based on severity, intent, and prior history.
4. Sanction is applied and documented in the employee's file.
5. Remedial training is assigned if appropriate.
6. Sanction records are retained for 6 years.

### 6.3 Non-Retaliation

{ORGANIZATION_NAME} prohibits retaliation against any workforce member who reports a security concern or policy violation in good faith.

## 7. Offboarding and Termination — 164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C)

### 7.1 Voluntary Departure

On or before the employee's last day:

1. Revoke all system access, including VPN, email, cloud services, and physical access.
2. Collect all {ORGANIZATION_NAME} property: devices, badges, keys, and tokens.
3. Disable the user account (do not delete — retain for audit purposes for {ACCOUNT_RETENTION_PERIOD}).
4. Remind the employee of ongoing confidentiality obligations.
5. Confirm offboarding completion in {OFFBOARDING_CHECKLIST_LOCATION}.

### 7.2 Involuntary Termination

In addition to the above:

- Access must be revoked simultaneously with or before notification of termination.
- For high-risk terminations (e.g., disgruntled employees, those with admin access), revoke access before the termination meeting.
- Conduct a post-termination audit of the user's recent ePHI access activity.
- Change any shared credentials or system passwords the individual had access to.

### 7.3 Contractor and Temporary Staff

- Business associate agreements or confidentiality agreements must be in place before access is granted.
- Contractor access must have a defined end date and be reviewed at contract renewal.
- Offboarding procedures apply equally to contractors and temporary staff.
